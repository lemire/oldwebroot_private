MATH 3423

Daniel Lemire, Ph.D.

Course Info (Winter 2002)

Numerical differentiation and integration, numerical solution of differential equations, optimization. The solution of problems on computer forms an integral part of the course.

TextBook

(To Be Confirmed!!!)

Michael T. Heath, Scientific Computing: An Introductory Survey, 2nd Edition (McGraw-Hill, 2001).

COURSE OUTLINE

Numerical differentiation and integration (2 weeks)

  • Numerical Quadrature
  • Integral Equations
  • Finite Difference

optimization (4 weeks)

  • Steepest Descent
  • Newton's Method
  • Linear and Nonlinear Least Square Methods
  • Linear programming

Numerical solution of differential equations (part 1) (4 weeks)

  • Euler's Method
  • Taylor Series Methods
  • Runge-Kutta Methods

Advanced Numerical solution of differential equations (part 2 -- as time permit!) (5 weeks)

  • Finite Difference Method
  • Galerkin
  • Finite Elements Method

Grading Scheme

4 Assignments 20%
Mid-Term Exam 20%
Final Exam 55%

Assignments will consist mostly of challenging problems to be solved using Matlab or any other convenient programming environment (C++ for example). In general, students will have two weeks to submit a report on the problem with plots and tables. Students may work in teams of up to 2 or work alone. In all cases, each student must write his own report. Students will be encouraged to use a word processor or similar tools (TeX, LaTeX...) to produce their reports.

Exams will consist of pen and paper questions and while they will be similar in content to the assignments, students are expected to be able to prove theorems (for example).

The first half of the course will be on Numerical integration and differential up to optimization problems. The Mid-Term Exam and the first two assignment will cover that material. We will then spend the rest of the course solving differential equations. The last two assignments will be on differential equations.

PREREQUISITES

  • Math 2413.
  • Strongly suggested : MATH 2023 or a comparable background in Differential Equations.
